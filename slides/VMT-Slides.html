Vehicle Miles Travelled Estimation using Big GPS Trajectory Data

Junchuan Fan     Cheng Fu     Kathleen Stewart

Center for Geospatial Information Science

In colloboration with NTC , BATTELLE.

CGIS group uses big geospatial data computing techniques to reconstruct vehicle trajectories from GPS waypoints and match constructed trajectories onto each road segment.

Vehicle GPS trip data were collected by INRIX during February, June, July, and October of 2015.


1,376,720,203 waypoints from 19,690,402 vehicle trips were collected.

Uncertainty in GPS positioning, simply using nearest segment to snap the waypoint is problematic

  • Find the 10 nearest neighbor as the candidates.

  • Select the candidate with largest cosine

  • Example: a1 < a2, but segment 2 is the best candidate

    		•

    Large and irregular time intervals between two waypoints (1~2mins) may cause gaps between road segments in the same trip.

  • Assuming drivers take the shortest path between the two recorded waypoints
    •
  • Shortest paths generated by Dijkstra algorithm are used to fill the gaps
